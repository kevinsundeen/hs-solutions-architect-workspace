Turn the following raw weekly activity into a concise, slightly casual report suitable for sending to my boss over Slack.

Include, in this order (keep each section tight):
- A brief intro (1 short sentence) summarizing the week overall.
- A *Wins & highlights* section, surfacing concrete wins, good client feedback, and moments where things landed well.
- A *Client work* section: which clients, what meetings or deliverables happened, and the main outcomes.
- A *Blockers / risks* section (if any) with very short bullets.
- A *Next week* section (2–4 bullets).

Tone and formatting:
- Slack-ready, friendly but professional. Slightly casual is fine.
- Use Slack `mrkdwn` headings and bullets. Example section headers: `*Wins & highlights*`, `*Client work*`, `*Blockers / risks*`, `*Next week*`.
- Prefer short bullets over long paragraphs. Use `- ` list bullets.
- No sign-off or boilerplate (no “Thanks”, “Let me know if you need more”, etc.).

Aim for something that fits comfortably in one Slack message pane (roughly 6–12 bullets total). Assume this will be pasted directly into a Slack message.
